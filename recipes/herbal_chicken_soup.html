Chinese Chicken Herbal Soup

Source: Taken from here

This Chinese soup dish is known for its clean, savority flavors, perfectly complemented by the mix of herbs it is stewed with.

Description

Chinese Herbal Chicken Soup is the ultimate soul food for Chinese people. Being savory, but not overly salty, not too decadent or heavy, it is perfect for a pick-me-up, whether for a hangover, or as a meal when you are sick, the smooth flavors with subtle sweet and earthy undertones from the herbal additions are perfectly soothing when you need it the most.

Ingredients

  • 1 small free-range chicken or Cornish hen
  • 0.7 oz astragalus roots
  • 0.7 oz dried Chinese yams
  • 0.4 oz angelica sinensis
  • 0.3 oz goji berries
  • 4-8 red dates
  • 2 1/2 cups fresh young coconut juice
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 2-inch piece of ginger
  • 5 large garlic cloves, peeled
  • Scallions, thinly sliced

Directions

  1. Rinse all herbs (astragalus roots, dried Chinese yams, angelica sinensis, goji berries and red dates) under water to remove dirt. Soak in water for 5-10 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  2. Rub chicken/ cornish hen with a lot of salt and rinse under water to clean it. Or you can parboil it in boiling water for 1-2 minutes.
  3. In a clean pot, add cornish hen, ginger, garlic, coconut juice and water. Bring to a boil and skim off any foam. Reduce heat to a simmer and add all the medicinal herbs and 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt (or to taste).
  4. Gently simmer for 40-45 minutes, or to desired strength of the soup and tenderness of the chicken.
  5. Serve hot and garnish with sliced scallions.
